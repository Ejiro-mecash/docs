## Coverage overview

- NGN senders can reach 16 cross-border destinations spanning West, East, and Southern Africa plus key Euro-American and Asian destinations.
- MWK senders retain local payouts as well as a USD off-ramp for cross-border needs.
- USD senders can route funds globally via SWIFT or use dedicated local routes for SEPA and Asian markets.
- RWF senders can route funds to Nigeria, creating a bi-directional corridor with our NGN -> Rwanda route.
- Bank transfers dominate for cross-border activity, while mobile money remains the fastest option for Ghana, Ivory Coast, Kenya, and Malawi.

### Payment channels

- **Bank transfer**: Credit a traditional bank account using local banking networks. Ensure beneficiaries can accept local inward transfers and have completed any required KYC.
- **Mobile money**: Deliver funds to a mobile wallet that recipients control via their phone. Recipients should confirm their wallet is active and has sufficient limits to accept the payout.
